Project title

Exploring the effects of TERRA RNA on innate immune activation and mitochondria during the aging process

Abstract

TERRA (telomeric repeat-containing RNA) is a long non-coding RNA transcribed from telomeric repeats. I investigated how TERRA accumulates in UV-induced senescent cells and acts as a signal that activates innate immune responses through the MDA5 / RIG-I pathway, while simultaneously suppressing mitochondrial biogenesis via the PGC-1α → NRF1 / NRF2 → TFAM axis. The work connects telomere biology, the senescence-associated secretory phenotype, and metabolic decline into a single mechanistic story.

Why it matters

Two consequences from one molecule

The striking part of this story is that a single accumulating RNA does two seemingly opposite things at once. On one hand it switches on inflammation by mimicking a viral signal that the cell's MDA5/RIG-I sensors detect. On the other hand it sits upstream of a cascade that switches off the cell's power plants, throttling mitochondrial biogenesis.

Together, these explain two hallmarks of aging cells: chronic low-grade inflammation ("inflammaging") and metabolic decline, through one upstream cause.

Approach

The project combined a UV-induced senescence model with molecular readouts of the immune and mitochondrial pathways, and computational analysis of expression patterns. I'm now extending the computational side with deep-learning models that predict regulatory behavior directly from sequence, the focus of the Lab page.

Status

This work earned a Second Award at the Taiwan International Science Fair (TISF) 2026 as a National Representative, and selection as Taiwan Representative to MILSET Expo-Sciences Europe 2026. A first-author manuscript is in preparation, expected 2027.
